Lean for the Curious Mathematician 2023

How can we be sure that our proofs are correct? Proof assistants are computer programs that promise to answer this question. They have now reached a level that makes the complete formalization, verification and digital processing of large parts of our mathematical knowledge seem within reach. Many successful formalization projects show that it is now possible to digitally represent and verify deep mathematical theorems with reasonable effort. In the future, proof assistants and digital proof libraries will likely become an important tool for mathematicians.

Lean for the Curious Mathematician 2023 is composed of two events happening in the week of 4.-8. September at the Heinrich Heine University Düsseldorf:

LftCM Tutorial

The goal of the Lean for the Curious Mathematician Tutorial is to introduce mathematicians without any prior knowledge in computer-assisted proofs to the proof assistant Lean. Expert lecturers will introduce the software and the associated mathematical library, and there will be plenty of time to try Lean in guided hands-on sessions. The number of participants is restricted and registration is required.

LftCM Colloquium

The subsequent Lean for the Curious Mathematician Colloquium will be a series of talks about computer-assisted proofs for mathematicians without prior experience. It will give an impression of the current developments in computer-assisted theorem proving and reflect on its future role in mathematics. The talks will be open to the public without registration and available online.
