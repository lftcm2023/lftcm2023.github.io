Lean for the Curious Mathematician 2023

Interactive theorem proving software has reached a level that makes the complete formalization, verification and digital processing of large parts of our mathematical knowledge seem within reach. Many successful formalization projects show that it is now possible to digitally represent and verify deep mathematical theorems with reasonable effort. In the future, proof assistants and digital proof libraries will likely become an important tool for mathematicians.

The goal of the Lean for the Curious Mathematician Tutorial is to introduce mathematicians without any prior knowledge in computer-assisted proofs to the proof assistant Lean.

The subsequent Lean for the Curious Mathematician Colloquium will give an overview of the current developments in computer-assisted theorem proving and reflect on its future role in mathematics.

Dates: 4.-8. September 2023

Venue: Heinrich Heine University Düsseldorf Düsseldorf

The event will take place at the main university campus, which is just ten minutes from the city centre by tram.

What Is Lean?

Lean is a proof assistant. It is a software to write down mathematical proofs and theories in a form that the computer can verify.
